I.

A being who could dream anything.

Once, there was a being who could dream anything. Not just at night, but at will. This being — call it God, or the Universe, or simply the Self — was alone in an endless, formless vastness. And so, for fun, it decided to dream a world.

II.

For eons, this was bliss.

It imagined galaxies swirling into birth, supernovas exploding like fireworks, oceans crashing against unseen shores. It built cities of crystal, loved without loss, and conquered every challenge before a single bead of sweat could form.

III.

Then came the boredom.

The being realized: I already know how every story ends. There is no surprise. No risk. No moment when my heart truly races. Slowly at first, then all at once — the emptiness crept in.

IV.

So it decided to add a little danger.

It dreamed a monster in the shadows. It dreamed a cliff with no guardrail. It dreamed betrayal, illness, and the bite of winter. And oh — that worked, for a while. The heart pounded. The senses sharpened.

V.

But even danger became predictable.

Because deep down, the dreamer always knew: I am the one who dreamed the monster. I will survive. I always do. And the boredom returned — heavier than before.

VI.

Its cleverest, strangest, most beautiful trick.

What if I forget that I am dreaming? What if I give myself real stakes — pain that feels final, love that feels fragile, time that feels like it's running out? And so the being did exactly that.

The Climax

The danger was real now.

Every scrape on a knee felt like a small tragedy. Every goodbye felt like it might be forever. Every choice felt heavy with consequence. The being — now living as you, as me — struggled, failed, triumphed, and wept without ever knowing it had set the stage.

The dreamer had finally hidden itself so well that even it couldn't find the exit.

VII.

And it will laugh — not bitterly.

With deep, warm delight. It will open its eyes in that formless vastness, stretch like a cat after a long nap, and whisper: Oh. That was me. All of it. The monster and the hero, the wound and the kiss, the terror and the peace.

VIII.

You are the universe experiencing itself.

You chose this. The uncertainty. The weight. The beauty of it. This moment. This life. This is the adventure you asked for — the dream you designed so perfectly that you forgot you were dreaming.

IX.

One day, the dreamer will wake up.

It will rest for a while. Float in the silence. And after a time, when the silence itself begins to feel a little empty…

X.

It will close its eyes.

And when the silence fills with longing again — for texture, for surprise, for the particular ache of being alive — it will do the only thing it knows.

And dream again.
